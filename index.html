Finnish Politicians Are Talking Less About Climate Change

By Sara Silvennoinen

For these visualizations, I've analyzed plenary speeches made by Finnish politicians in parliament during 2015-2025, scraping data from the official eduskunta.fi website, and analyzing it using Pandas. Using keyword searches related to climate and environmental issues, such as "ilmastonmuutos" (climate change) and "luonnon monimuotoisuus" (biodiversity). I tracked how frequently these topics appeared in parliamentary speeches over time.

The analysis shows that mentions of climate change and environmental concerns have declined in recent years. The outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 and the onset of the war in Ukraine in 2022 shifted parliamentary focus away from some of the environmental issues.

The chart below shows which Finnish political parties most frequently mentioned "climate change" in their parliamentary speeches during 2024. The Social Democratic Party leads the discussion, referencing climate change 54 times, followed by the Green League with 40 mentions. Centre Party, Left Alliance, and the Finns Party also address the topic, though less frequently. In contrast, parties such as the Christian Democratic Party and Movement Now make only a handful of references.
